Dyno Movement Breakdown and Analysis

A detailed breakdown and description of the dyno

Skill Discussion

As discussed in the video, the dyno is a foundational skill for parkour athletes. Not only is it an explosive movement, but also incorperates many functional elements present in other movements such as a strong knee drive, upper body and lower body co-ordinated movement, an upper body pull and a lowering motion. For these reasons, we use the dyno as a baseline testing movement for competitive athletes.

When we perform a baseline test for the skill, an athlete will begin in a cat position on a protruding ledge with blank wall above them. Paper or other markable material is placed on the wall. The fingertips of both hands are covered with a washable ink before the athlete initiates the movement. Then, the athlete dynos as high as they can, reaching for the wall above. The distance between the top of the starting block and the highest mark left on the paper is then measured and recorded. If the athlete's hands do not reach the same height above the starting point the lowest of the marks left on the paper are measured and recorded.
